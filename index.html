01Executive Summary

Over a 24-hour observation period, the Cowrie SSH honeypot recorded 2,841 login attempts originating from 384 unique IP addresses across 19 countries. The majority of traffic originates from China (25.5%), Russia (19.8%), and the United States (14.1%), consistent with known automated scanning and botnet infrastructure.

Of the 127 established sessions, 3 sessions (2.4%) demonstrated persistent attacker behavior with Attacker Persistence Scores above 70, indicating human or semi-automated post-exploitation activity. One session (IP: 185.220.101.42) achieved root access and attempted XMRig crypto-miner installation, cron-based persistence, and anti-forensics cleanup — the most sophisticated attack pattern observed.

02Attacker Behavior Analysis

Behavioral classification via the Attacker Persistence Score (APS) formula — APS = session_duration + command_count + login_attempts — reveals four distinct attacker archetypes:

PERSISTENT (APS > 70) · 2 sessions
Manual or semi-automated actors. Long dwell time, structured command chains, explicit post-exploitation goals.
BRUTE-FORCE (APS 40–70) · 28 sessions
Automated credential sprayers. High attempt counts, libssh2/paramiko client signatures, no post-login interaction.
AUTO-SCANNER (APS 15–40) · 61 sessions
Masscan/Shodan-type probes. Default credential check, immediate disconnect. High volume, low persistence.
PORT SCANNER (APS < 15) · 36 sessions
Banner grab or version detection only. No authentication attempted. Likely reconnaissance phase of broader campaign.

04Threat Intelligence Findings

AbuseIPDB enrichment confirms that the top three attacking IPs have established malicious histories with a combined 10,171 abuse reports. IP 185.220.101.42 is a known Tor exit node in the Netherlands, routinely used to anonymize SSH brute-force campaigns and botnet C2 traffic. The XMRig download URL pattern is consistent with the "XMRig-Deploy" campaign tracked by multiple threat intelligence feeds since Q3 2023. The use of libssh2 as the SSH client is a strong automated tooling indicator.
